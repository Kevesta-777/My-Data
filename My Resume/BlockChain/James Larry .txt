1. Jleague 
• Description 
One of the projects I participated in is the J.League platform, which you can explore at J.League.com. This platform allows users to view the performance of football teams and the status of players during the current game season in Japan. 
 In this project, I was responsible for both the frontend and backend development. We utilized Django and PostgreSQL for the frontend, backend, and database management. I mentored three frontend developers and personally built most of the user interfaces. 
 To ensure pixel-perfect UIs, we maintained flexible communication with the designers. After two months of development, the frontend developers' speed increased significantly, which led to a bottleneck on the backend side. Consequently, I mentored the junior developers and took charge of the backend development simultaneously. 
 As a result, we completed the development and delivered the product within three months from the project's inception. 
  
• Risk 
The primary challenge in this project was reducing the page loading time. Unlike frameworks such as React, Vue, and Angular, Django renders entire pages rather than building them by component. This posed a significant issue as the page content included numerous images and videos, resulting in substantial loading times. 
 To address this, I implemented AJAX to load each part of the page asynchronously. Additionally, I optimized the database structure and queries to enhance search speed. 
After these two steps, the loading time was reduced from 5 minutes to 3 or 5 seconds.
